# Participant Guide — AI in Education: Central Tensions

Welcome to the MPCR Labs Round Table. Here's how to participate using your phone.

---

## Getting Started

### 1. Scan the QR code

Use your phone's camera to scan the QR code on your table (or on the projector screen). It opens in your browser — no app needed.

### 2. Check in

Enter:
- **Your name** — this is how you'll appear in comments (you can also post anonymously)
- **Your role** — Educator, Researcher, Policymaker, Student, or Other
- **Your table number** — check the card on your table

Tap **JOIN SESSION**.

### 3. You're in

Your phone now shows the current topic and your participation tools. The big screen shows everyone's responses in real time.

---

## How It Works

We'll discuss four tensions — one at a time. For each tension:

### Vote on the spectrum

When voting opens, you'll see five buttons between two poles — for example:

```
Cognitive offloading  ◄────►  Deep learning
   [1]  [2]  [3]  [4]  [5]
```

Tap the number that best represents your position:
- **1** = strongly agree with the left side
- **3** = neutral / it depends
- **5** = strongly agree with the right side

Your vote appears as a dot on the big screen along with everyone else's.

### Ask a question

Type a question in the **Questions** box and tap **Ask**. Your question appears for everyone. Others can upvote it by tapping the ▲ button.

The facilitator sees the most upvoted questions and may read them to the room.

### Post a comment

Type a thought in the **Comments** box and tap **Post**.

By default, comments are **anonymous** (the toggle says "Anonymous" in gold). Tap the toggle to post with your name instead.

### Table discussion

When voting closes, your phone shows a **table notepad**. This is shared with everyone at your table. Use it to capture your table's key points or summary.

Anyone at the table can type — the notes save automatically.

### Revote

After your table discusses, the facilitator may open a **revote**. You'll see:

> *REVOTE — has your position changed?*

Your original vote is shown. Tap a new position if your thinking shifted, or tap the same one if it didn't.

The big screen shows how the whole room shifted — blue dots (before) and gold dots (after discussion).

## The Four Tensions

| # | Left pole | Right pole |
|---|-----------|------------|
| T1 | Cognitive offloading | Deep learning |
| T2 | Personalization | Belonging |
| T3 | Assistance | Authorship |
| T4 | Innovation | Governance |

Each tension asks: where should the balance be? There is no right answer. The point is to surface disagreements and understand why they exist.

---

## Tips

- **Vote honestly.** The spectrum is not a test. Extreme positions are valuable data.
- **Ask questions you genuinely wonder about.** The best questions are the ones you don't know the answer to.
- **Upvote other people's questions.** The most upvoted questions get discussed by the room.
- **Use the table notepad.** Your table's insights are worth capturing. One person can type while others talk.
- **Change your mind.** The revote exists because good discussion should shift thinking. Moving your position is a sign of learning, not weakness.

## FAQ

**Do I need to download an app?**
No. It works in your phone's browser. Just scan and go.

**Can I see who voted what?**
No. Votes are anonymous. The big screen shows dots without names.

**Can I change my vote?**
During the initial voting period, you can tap a different number to change it. During the revote phase, you can set a new position. Between those phases, your vote is locked.

**What if my phone dies?**
Raise your hand during the vote. The facilitator can note your position manually.

**Is my data stored?**
Votes and questions are stored temporarily on statevec.com and auto-expire after 24 hours. The facilitator may export a summary for research purposes. Your name is only attached to comments where you chose to post non-anonymously.

**Can I participate after the event?**
The SVG files remain functional. If you save or bookmark the URL, you can revisit, but the data expires after 24 hours.

This event uses **living documents** — SVG files that coordinate through shared state. Your phone, the facilitator's laptop, and the projector are all reading and writing to the same shared space. No server, no app, no accounts. Just files in a browser.
